Drip, drip, drip…

Mon Feb 29 2016 10:25:34 GMT+1100 (AEDT) Aryan Verma

I came home late in the evening and found out that mum and dad were out of town for some party. Well, I was a bit afraid of being alone in the house since it was double storey with five large bedrooms. I turned on the radio and found myself a snack, and just then the music stopped and a deep voice came on and warned everyone living in the suburban area to lock all doors and close every window because there was a murderer on the loose. I quickly ran to every single door and window and locked them making sure the curtains were closed. But I didn’t worry too much because I had my faithful dog Spotless with me. We have been together since I was a little baby and both of us trust each other a lot.

I went to bed and Spotless curled up right under my bed. I dropped my hand down and Spotless licked it. Whenever he licks me I feel comfortable. I gave a sigh and fell asleep. After an hour or so I heard a faint noise from downstairs, but I ignored it. I let my hand fall on the side of the bed and he licked it. Not long after I fell asleep again I heard footsteps outside and I thought it might be mum and dad, so I stepped outside but saw no one. I went back inside and fell asleep with a lick on my hand. After a few hours I woke up again and heard a dripping sound. Probably it was from the kitchen tap. Fed up of the noises, I went downstairs and tightened the tap. I again went to sleep with a lick on my hand. Later I again woke up to a dripping sound and figured it must be from the bathroom. When I opened the door and turned on the lights I was frozen with fear.

From the ceiling of the bathroom, Spotless was hanging with blood dripping from his throat, and on the mirror there was a message: ‘Humans can lick too…’
