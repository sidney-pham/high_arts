Descent

Mon Feb 29 2016 10:25:10 GMT+1100 (AEDT) Someone

I look around,

Seeing nothing.

Only blurs of movement,

Muted by shades of despair.

Bound to a stake of thorns,

Each wilted petal like stabbing death.

Arms shackled over hanging head

Like a lifeless puppet

I drop.

A burdened anchor plunging into sinking blackness.

The darkness wraps itself around me,

Like a curtain wringing the light out.

There I lie,

On beds of sleepless water.

Wanting to self-destruct

As burning waves of damning scorn

Echoing with self-pity

Ravage me of my will to live.

Leaving behind its calling card of tears.

I wander,

my haunted house

Searching for old photos among fresh ashes

Stumbling on candles I drop.

A prisoner in an empty cell

Chained to harbouring sorrow.

My appetite starved by grief

Choked by the quicksands of melancholy.

Mirrors shatter,

Jaded with sympathy,

Cascade like hungry guillotine.

Fighting,

I fly like a bird through the night

On clipped wings of brilliant orange,

I fall.
