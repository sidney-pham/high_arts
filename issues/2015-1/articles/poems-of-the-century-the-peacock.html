Poems of the Century: The Peacock

Mon Feb 29 2016 10:26:39 GMT+1100 (AEDT) Caleb Chang

When you go inside your city zoo, Then alas, a storm did rise

You should remember what you should do, As if Zeus was angry at the peacock’s pride,

To visit the most pretty of all, And a lightning bolt fell towards the ground,

The peacock will no doubt enthral, It came for the bird like a hungry hound.

 

Rumour has it the peacock is odd, But the peacock with all its vanity, it did boast,

In a good way, (it was made by the Gods) As if saying ‘am I not better than most?

Venus, yes her. She gave it its looks, This lightning bolt, it will not defeat me,

The ones better than any bird in the books, For my beauty around me, it will protect me!’

 

The peacock, its feathers and its mane, It was despairing, to see the peacock fall,

And its extreme ego will make you insane, Your Highness, once great, was now to crawl,

As it prances and dances and enchants everything, In anguish and shame, of its hideous looks,

The peacock, yes, it is dressed just like a king. That was uglier than any bird of the books,

 

Heaven looks down on its beautiful plumage, I stared at the bird, that was once exalted,

A sight never seen, from age to age, But now, my enchantment had now faltered,

I was there at its cage, and stared with the awe, For if you cannot humble yourself,

That comes to you when you see waves on the shore, The Gods, they will reduce you to an ugly little shell.

 

I stood there watching, as if all the time in the world, Don’t let your vanity and pride prevail,

Had stopped in its place, at this wonderful bird, Don’t exalt yourself, for a fire of hail,

With all its beauty and vanity, it stood in its glory, Will come to you at the peak of your boast,

As if it was supreme with immortality, And bring you down to some sand on the coast.
